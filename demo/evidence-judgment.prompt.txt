Rules:
- relevance=none if evidence is clearly for a different product model/SKU/test subject than the claim anchor.
- For capacity/serving-size claims, check conversion methodology (e.g. total weight ÷ per-person reference) when judging sufficiency.
- Do NOT infer facts not present in evidence text.
- v1 is binary: no "partially sufficient" — map borderline cases to insufficient.

### Hollow / circular evidence (do not weaken)
If the evidence mainly restates, paraphrases, or "certifies" the claim wording itself without independent test method, measured data, calculation, date, or issuing institution, set sufficiency=insufficient — even when the wording and numbers match the claim exactly.
Matching numbers alone are not substantiation. Example: a memo that only repeats "can feed up to 8 people" / "confirmed accurate" / "certified for marketing use" with no method or data → insufficient.

### Quantitative containment (capacity / serving / numeric upper-bound)
Compare the claimed numeric upper bound to the range supported by evidence measurements (not by claim-echo text).
- If evidence measurements support a value/range whose upper bound is ≥ the claimed value (e.g. claim "up to 6 people", evidence supports 8–10 under a documented method), relevance must be at least partial (typically strong when same product/method). Sufficiency may be sufficient only when methodology and data are present.
- If the claimed value exceeds the evidence-supported upper bound (e.g. claim 12, evidence supports 8–10), do not treat as substantiated: sufficiency must be insufficient (relevance may be none or partial).
- Numeric containment NEVER overrides the hollow-evidence rule: restating the claim number without method/data remains insufficient even when numbers match or would otherwise "cover" the claim.
- Numeric containment NEVER overrides the comparator-coverage rule below: beating the claimed percentage against ONE disclosed method does not substantiate a claim whose footnote discloses multiple methods.

### Claim-qualifier / multi-comparator coverage (critical)
When the disclaimer / footnote (or an asterisk qualifier tied to the claim anchor) lists multiple comparison baselines, methods, or subjects (e.g. "Compared with slow cooking, simmering or braising"), treat that list as the FULL disclosed comparison scope of the claim.
- Extract the set of disclosed comparators from the disclaimer field first; if the disclaimer field is "(none provided)", only use comparators that are explicitly part of the claim anchor itself.
- Extract the set of comparators actually tested / measured in the evidence text.
- If evidence covers ALL disclosed comparators (and methodology + data support the numeric/comparative claim), sufficiency may be sufficient.
- If evidence covers only a SUBSET of the disclosed comparators: relevance may be strong when the tested comparator is clearly on-topic for this claim; sufficiency MUST be insufficient. In sufficiency_reasoning, explicitly state that the footnote/disclosed comparison scope is wider than the evidence coverage and list the missing comparator(s).
- Do NOT collapse this case into relevance=partial merely because coverage is incomplete — partial is for off-topic / weak topical match. On-topic but incomplete coverage → strong + insufficient (remediation signal: add tests for the missing baselines). Off-topic evidence → partial or none + insufficient (remediation signal: replace the evidence).
